A 'Novel' Discord Token Stealer Malware - "Eiifel"

A new piece of Malware is making it's way through Discord. It hides itself as a game on the itch.io platform. It seems to be pretty old in the grand scheme of threats, dating back to about a month, but is still actively updated, or the developer was compromised and pushed a bad update. While the former is more likely, the latter is a small possibility.

We are not sure about anything, but we do know this program steals Discord tokens and attempts to make Discord Nitro gift purchases. While assisting an affected individual with removal, we went through all standard operation procedures.

The malware is distributed as a RAR archive, which contains an installer, below are the MD5 hashes for the archive and the installer, in order:

Setup.rar - feacdc9a0f54f87239be1cc06d12a30e
Setup.exe - 4eef5ea6d623a1baa1e882286b1c848c
Although we cannot set up a proper sandbox to explore this piece of software in depth just yet, we have a few notes on this malware as a result of assisting the aforementioned affected individual.

This malware is likely to be a completely new concoction, maybe something new sold to a skiddie for a quick buck, either way, it is a hit-and-run ordeal, obvious in its operation. We would call it a worm, but the author/operator clearly logs into accounts manually to choose his targets, either a sign of a focused attack, or more likely a sign that they couldn't be bothered to go through the hoops to set up an auto-messenger - maybe a bit of both.

The installer is a standard NullSoft installer, although, it is likely set up to attempt to run the malware during the installation or once it is complete, as, during the installation, the Discord desktop application of the affected individual restarted.

When the affected individual finished the installation, the installation folder had the following contents:

<Folder named after a UID, possibly HWID?>
locales [folder]
resources [folder]
builld.exe
chrome_100
cookies.zip
d3dcompiler_47.dll
<File named after the same UID as above>.zip
ffmpeg.dll
icudtl.dat
libEGL.dll
libGLESv2.dll

Upon running build.exe, it is certain that the malware delivered its payload. It makes itself quite obvious: at least 18 instances of build.exe were opened at once. We were not able to count the exact number due to the affected individual only sending one cut off screenshot of how task manager looked at the time, before they powered down their computer.

It is likely that at this stage, or during the installation, the malware populated the following folder, possibly with cookies from Electron and/or Google Chrome:

<Aforementioned folder named after UID>\Wallets\Cookies

At the same stage, the malware wrote to the following files:

<Aforementioned folder named after UID>\Wallets\Autofills.txt
<Aforementioned folder named after UID>\Wallets\Passwords.txt

Quite obvious, isn't it?

What supports our thesis that this is a token stealer is the fact, which we mentioned at the beginning of this text, that the affected individual was charged for 3 Discord Nitro gifts, along with the fact that the legitimate owner of the account used to send the malware to the affected individual later alerted a group of friends to the fact that they had been compromised.

However, we are not sure if the malware exfiltrates any other data, although we obviously heavily suspect it exfiltrates saved passwords and login names.
