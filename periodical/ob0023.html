TEXT BY THE REVOLUTIONARY TECHNICAL COMMITTEE IN THE BRITISH ISLES
On the Scottish National Party
After Nicola Sturgeon’s announcement of her resignation as leader of the Scottish National Party on the 15th of February, the expected front-runner to replace her, Kate Forbes, wasted no time in proclaiming her bigotry everywhere she could be heard. When this first began, many expected it to sink her campaign, but she has proven far more resilient, topping multiple polls of SNP voters for their preferred leader (although Don’t Know remains firmly in the lead). Whilst many have reacted with surprise and dismay at this fact, as the SNP’s outwardly progressive standpoint (considering the fact that, in recent months they have proven themselves by far the least transphobic of the UK’s major parties) had led to many to believe that such a homophobic candidate could not hope to succeed. In reality, however, this is entirely expected for any nationalist organisation, no matter how deeply they have wrapped themselves in progressive-liberal or social-democratic ideas.

The proponents of the Scottish nationalist movement have, over the years, taken great pains to cast themselves in history not as the beneficiaries of and enthusiastic participants in the colonial brutality of the British Empire, but instead as noblefreedom fighters, celts oppressed for centuries by theperfidious English. This is blatantly historical revisionism - the Scottish were as closely tied to the Empire as any of the English, and where they were overshadowed in importance it was due not to a Scottish opposition to participation in colonialexploitation but simply a fact of their smaller population compared to England.

Yet, the nationalists in Scotland ignore such facts, casting themselves as an imperialised people that attempts to evoke the echoes of those actually imperialised- the idea of a “Celtic Union” with the Irish (who were, of course, victims of Scottish colonialism in Ulster) is one such example. Scottish nationalism is therefore one of the most cynically bourgeois “national liberation” movements in the modern world: where most national liberation movements at least involve at least some level of marginalisation which national movements can feed off of to grow support, Scottish nationalism revolves largely around wanting admission to an imperialist trade bloc, as well as some hand-wringing about perceived “democratic deficit” within the UK (the pointlessness of democracy aside, those in Scotland have far more opportunities to vote for their government than those in England).
