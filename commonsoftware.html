Our new position on Common Software
While it originated as a concept with good intentions, we believe Common Software does more harm than good in the scenario we find ourselves in.

A particular problem that has always permeated the technology field is that of a complete reverence towards bourgeois legalism: for every programmer up-to-date with the latest trend, EULAs, licenses and other useless pieces of paper have become their new gods. It makes it an impossibility for them to look past the liberal-democratic legal system, and translates itself into unwillingness to participate in strikes and other actions to further the interests of their class, into the obedience perpetrated by the bourgeois compromises and regime unionism.

We believe that, by introducing another piece of paper to the pile with the Commons Protection License, and by creating a whole concept around it, we have done more harm than good. After deep self-criticism, we have decided to discontinue Common Software as a whole, so that there is no confusion between it and our actual programmatic content.

As for our position on whatever license you should use for your work: use whatever you think is best, but always with awareness that the license you choose is only valid for those who have the power to defend the so-called "rights" and "freedoms" they grant. There is no harm in open-sourcing code, but doing it isn't a gigantic statement.

For historical purposes (and, if you still want to use it), the Commons Protection License remains archived, but deprecated.
